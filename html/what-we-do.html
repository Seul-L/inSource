What We Do

At inSource our goal is to help create an open and collaborative environment for solving problems within your organization.

We value privacy

Our two-part service offers an online platform for anonymous submissions that will give your organization insight into the key issues being faced by employees, and an in-person Sprint Workshop that will empower your people to rapidly solve the issue at hand.

We value communication

We also recognize that coming up with great solutions is the easy part. Implementing and communicating the progress is often where solutions enter the idea graveyard. This is where inSource’s follow-up process comes in. After your organization has participated in a Sprint Workshop your inSource Community Manager will work hand-in-hand with your project team to communicate progress and provide updates to the rest of the organization.

We value our process

Part A

During the submission phrase of the process, members will be invited to submit issues and concerns they wish to have addressed by their management. Members can submit up to 5 different issues.

After the submission period is completed, the Community Manager will work to aggregate and group similar submissions. Topics will be formed based on the issues at the core of your employees' submissions.

Part B

During the vote phase of the process topics will be posted and members will be invited to zen vote on which specific topics they want addressed in the Sprint Workshop.

After the vote phase is complete the inSource team will identify which topic will be the focus for the upcoming Sprint Workshop. The Sprint Workshop topic will be announced and members will be randomly selected to receive an invite to the workshop.

Part C

The last part of the inSource process is designing a solution to the issue identified in the voting phase. To develop this solution the inSource team uses a tested sprint methodology that will take your organization from issue to solution with a plan for implementation in just one day.

The one day Sprint Workshop will feature representatives from across your organization, as well as an inSource Sprint Master to guide you through the process. Our inSource Sprint Master is an experienced facilitator who will coach and guide the group through the Sprint Workshop.

The goal at the end of the Sprint Workshop is for your team to walk away with a viable solution to the challenge they started with.

Follow Up

After the Sprint Workshop has taken place the next step in the inSource process is to communicate the results of the Sprint to the rest of the organization. These results will be posted as updates on the inSource web portal for everyone in the organization to view and see the current progress. Throughout the implementation the inSource team will keep in contact with the project team at your organization to communicate milestones and updates to your employees.

The Follow-up is one of the most important parts of the inSource process - it allows all members to be engaged, regardless of their participation in the Sprint Workshop and also provides insight into the proposed solution and the actual outcome.
By using the inSource platform, organizations can directly engage their employees by discovering what issues and concerns they are facing. From there, the organization has the ability to empower their employees to solve those exact problems they feel are important. Employees will not only feel more engaged and supported by their employers, but employers are able to rapidly solve organizational challenges. Solutions which will have a direct impact for the organization. Who better to solve your organization’s issues than people within your organization? Why outsource when you can inSource!
